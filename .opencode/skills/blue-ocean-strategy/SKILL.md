# Blue Ocean Strategy Frameworks

> Create uncontested market space and make the competition irrelevant through value innovation.

---

## 1. Core Concept: Red Ocean vs. Blue Ocean

| Red Ocean Strategy | Blue Ocean Strategy |
|-------------------|---------------------|
| Compete in existing market space | Create uncontested market space |
| Beat the competition | Make competition irrelevant |
| Exploit existing demand | Create and capture new demand |
| Make value-cost trade-off | Break value-cost trade-off |
| Align for differentiation OR low cost | Align for differentiation AND low cost |

**Value Innovation**: The simultaneous pursuit of differentiation and low cost. Not about innovation for its own sake, but about creating a leap in value for buyers and your company.

## 2. Strategy Canvas

Visual diagnostic and action framework that captures the current state of play and shows how to create a compelling blue ocean.

```
Offering Level
     ↑
High │                              /‾‾‾‾‾ You (Blue Ocean)
     │                             /
     │  Competitor A ─────────────/        
     │         \              /
Med  │          \            /
     │           \          /
     │  Competitor B ──────/      Competitor C
     │
Low  │____________________________________________→
        Factor 1   Factor 2   Factor 3   Factor 4   Factor 5
                   Factors of Competition
```

### Building Your Strategy Canvas

**Step 1: Identify Competing Factors**
- List factors the industry competes on (price, features, service, etc.)
- These are the x-axis elements

**Step 2: Plot Current Players**
- Rate each competitor on each factor (Low to High)
- Draw value curves for 2-3 key competitors
- Include your current position if applicable

**Step 3: Read the Strategic Landscape**
- Where do curves converge? (Red ocean - intense competition)
- Where do they diverge? (Potential blue ocean)
- What factors are over-served? Under-served?

**Step 4: Design Your Blue Ocean Curve**
- Use Four Actions Framework (below)
- Create a unique value curve
- Ensure clear divergence from competition

### Strategy Canvas Checklist
- [ ] Curve has clear divergence from competitors
- [ ] Focus is on big picture, not numbers
- [ ] Communicates strategic profile visually
- [ ] Can be explained in 1 minute
- [ ] Shows clear value proposition

## 3. Four Actions Framework (ERRC)

Reconstruct buyer value elements to craft a new value curve.

| Action | Question | Strategic Logic | Example |
|--------|----------|-----------------|---------|
| **ELIMINATE** | Which factors should be eliminated? | Factors industry takes for granted but no longer add value | Southwest eliminated meals, lounges, hub systems |
| **REDUCE** | Which factors should be reduced well below industry standard? | Over-designed features for current demand | Southwest reduced comfort, ticketing flexibility |
| **RAISE** | Which factors should be raised well above industry standard? | Factors that matter more than industry recognizes | Southwest raised speed, frequency, convenience |
| **CREATE** | Which factors should be created that industry never offered? | Sources of new value | Southwest created point-to-point, friendly service, price transparency |

### ERRC Grid Template

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                     ELIMINATE                               │
│  • Factor to remove                                         │
│  • Factor to remove                                         │
│  • Factor to remove                                         │
├──────────────────────────────────────┬──────────────────────┤
│            REDUCE                    │        RAISE         │
│  • Lower this factor                 │  • Increase this     │
│  • Lower this factor                 │  • Increase this     │
│  • Lower this factor                 │  • Increase this     │
├──────────────────────────────────────┴──────────────────────┤
│                     CREATE                                  │
│  • New factor to add                                        │
│  • New factor to add                                        │
│  • New factor to add                                        │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Applying ERRC

1. List all factors your industry competes on
2. For each factor, decide: Eliminate, Reduce, Raise, or Create
3. Eliminate/Reduce → Lower costs
4. Raise/Create → Increase buyer value
5. Result: Value innovation (differentiation + low cost)

## 4. Six Paths Framework

Systematically explore opportunities to create blue oceans across alternative industries and strategic groups.

### Path 1: Look Across Alternative Industries
- What are the alternatives to your industry?
- Example: Cirque du Soleil looked across theater + circus

### Path 2: Look Across Strategic Groups Within Industry
- What strategic groups exist? (e.g., budget vs. luxury)
- What drives buyers to trade up or down?
- Example: Lexus (luxury quality at near-luxury price)

### Path 3: Look Across Chain of Buyers
- Who is the chain? Purchaser → User → Influencer
- Can you target a different buyer in the chain?
- Example: Bloomberg targeted users (traders), not IT departments

### Path 4: Look Across Complementary Products/Services
- What happens before/after your product is used?
- What are the pain points in the total solution?
- Example: iTunes + iPod (music + device)

### Path 5: Look Across Functional-Emotional Orientation
- Is your industry functional or emotional?
- What if you flipped the orientation?
- Example: Swatch (functional watch → fashion accessory)

### Path 6: Look Across Time
- What trends will matter in 5-10 years?
- How will buyer values change?
- Example: iPhone anticipated mobile computing trend

## 5. Three Tiers of Noncustomers

Convert latent demand into real demand by understanding who isn't buying now.

### Tier 1: "Soon-to-be" Noncustomers
- Buyers on the edge of your market
- Waiting to jump ship
- **Action**: Keep them in, remove switching barriers

### Tier 2: "Refusing" Noncustomers
- Consciously chose against your industry
- Explored alternatives, opted out
- **Action**: Understand deal-breakers, eliminate them

### Tier 3: "Unexplored" Noncustomers
- Never considered your industry
- Served by alternatives
- **Action**: Understand their needs, unlock new demand

### Noncustomer Analysis

For each tier:
1. Who are they? (Demographics, psychographics)
2. Why don't they buy? (Key barriers)
3. What would unlock them? (Key appeals)
4. What's the market size? (Opportunity)

## 6. Fair Process

Build execution into strategy through engagement, explanation, and expectation clarity.

### Three Principles

**Engagement** - Involve people in decisions that affect them
- Ask for input
- Listen actively
- Incorporate ideas

**Explanation** - Everyone understands final decisions
- Why this direction?
- Why not alternatives?
- Connect to big picture

**Expectation Clarity** - Clear expectations of performance
- What needs to change?
- What stays the same?
- How success is measured?

## 7. Blue Ocean Shift Process

Systematic five-step process to move from red to blue ocean.

### Step 1: Get Started
- Choose a blue ocean team
- Build confidence through quick wins
- Define scope (product, service, unit)

### Step 2: Understand Where You Are
- Visual awakening (Strategy Canvas)
- See current state clearly
- Identify competition focus

### Step 3: Imagine Where You Could Be
- Six Paths exploration
- Noncustomer analysis
- Generate blue ocean ideas

### Step 4: Find How to Get There
- Apply Four Actions (ERRC)
- Design new Strategy Canvas
- Check for value innovation

### Step 5: Make Your Move
- Validate with buyers
- Build business case
- Execute with fair process

## 8. Validation Tests

Before committing, test your blue ocean idea:

### Buyer Utility Test
- Is there exceptional buyer utility?
- Does it solve a problem or create new utility?
- Is the price accessible to mass buyers?

### Price Test
- Can you profit at strategic pricing?
- Is cost structure sustainable?
- Can you hit target profit margin?

### Adoption Test
- What are adoption hurdles?
- Can you overcome them?
- Is education needed?

### Scale Test
- Is market large enough?
- Can you scale operations?
- Is business model viable long-term?

## 9. Common Pitfalls

❌ **It's just a branding exercise** - No, it's fundamental strategy redesign
❌ **Ignore cost side** - Must achieve both differentiation AND low cost
❌ **Focus on existing customers only** - Look at noncustomers
❌ **One-time exercise** - Markets evolve; revisit regularly
❌ **Skip validation** - Test assumptions with real buyers
❌ **Forget fair process** - Execution requires buy-in
